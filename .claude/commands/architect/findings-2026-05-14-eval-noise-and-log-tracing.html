Eval noise floor & the log-tracing weakness

Findings — 2026-05-14. Triggered by a byte-identical reproduction of the archived adas-subagent v0 run on mapgpt_mp3d. What looked like a clean evolutionary success turned out to be a walk on noise — and the investigation surfaced a structural gap in how the architect pipeline reads experiment logs.

scope all architect variants   graph mapgpt_mp3d   model gpt-5-mini-2025-08-07   eval MapGPT72 / 216 ep

TL;DR — three findings, one chain of causation.

1. The original archive's "beautiful monotonic chase" (iter_0 0.435 → iter_5 0.540, +0.105 SR) does not reproduce. Byte-identical reruns give iter_0 = 0.472 and iter_5 = 0.468 — the entire staircase is within the single-run noise band.

2. log.jsonl cannot be robustly reconstructed per-episode. Only 23 % of events carry any episode-identifying text; there is no episode_index / worker_id field.

3. No code in the architect pipeline parses log.jsonl. "Analyse the trajectory log" is a prose instruction handed to an LLM sub-agent, which improvises the same fragile reconstruction on a sample of ~8 of 216 episodes.

Together: the search hill-climbs on a fitness signal whose noise (±0.07–0.10) exceeds every per-step gain it ever recorded, and its failure-mode "analysis" is built on a thin, fragile, sampled view of the logs.

1. The reproduction that started it

The archived adas-subagent v0 run on mapgpt_mp3d showed an almost-monotonic SR climb across 5 evolved iterations. The full chain only ever edited two fields of mapgpt_mp3d.json — plan_step.stop_after (an integer, 3→4→4→5→5→5) and planner_llm.system_prompt (prose STOP-rule additions). No topology change, no nodeset code touched.

We reproduced iter_0 (baseline) and iter_5 (best) byte-identically: copied each iter's active_workspace/ from the archive, pinned the planner to profile: "gpt-5-mini" explicitly (verified in log.jsonl as gpt-5-mini-2025-08-07), same MapGPT72 216-episode split, same backend overlay path.

iterconfig original SRrepro SRΔ
iter_0baseline — stop_after=3, no prompt mods 0.4350.472+0.037
iter_5stop_after=5 + DESTINATION SIGNATURE + STOP GATE 0.5400.468−0.072

The staircase evaporated. Reproduced iter_0 (0.472) and reproduced iter_5 (0.468) are statistically indistinguishable. The original +0.105 climb was iter_0 rolling its lower tail plus iter_5 rolling its upper tail, with the four intermediate iters filling a plausible-looking ramp.

One repro went up, the other went down — which also rules out a systematic time-of-day / API-load effect (that would push both the same direction). It is symmetric sampling noise.

2. The changes were real — they just net to zero

Per-episode comparison of the two reproductions (216 common episodes, keyed by instruction):

metricvaluereading
Cohen's κ (per-episode success agreement) 0.415 far from 1.0 (identical behaviour) — the graphs genuinely differ
episodes that flipped outcome 63 / 216 (29 %) 32 success→fail, 31 fail→success — near-symmetric
aggregate SR 0.472 vs 0.468 net change ≈ 0

The step_count histogram confirms the structural change took effect: iter_0 stops at steps 4/5/6; iter_5 has zero stops at steps 4–5 (the stop_after=5 gate is physically enforced) and 64 episodes pile up at step 6, the first legal stop. iter_5 also walks further (traj_len 16.0→18.5) and reaches the goal region more often (oracle_success 0.602→0.634).

The zero-sum mechanism. The STOP GATE / stop_after=5 changes induce two opposing effects that cancel:

  • + no early stop → longer exploration → reaches the goal area more often (oracle_success +0.03)
  • − the two-gate interrogation makes the agent second-guess at the goal → worse final stop decision ("reached goal area but failed": 28 → 36)

coverage↑ − stop-precision↓ ≈ net-zero SR. Every prompt-engineering iter in the original chain changed agent behaviour — it just traded coverage against stop-precision without moving the success rate.

3. Quantifying the noise floor

quantityvalue
pure binomial SD of a single 216-ep run at p≈0.47 √(0.47·0.53/216) ≈ 0.034
observed repro range (iter_0: 0.037, iter_5: 0.072) effective SD ≈ 0.035–0.05
single-run 95 % CI half-width ±0.07–0.10

The LLM's temperature-1 sampling stochasticity stacks on top of the binomial noise, roughly doubling the effective SD. The gpt-5 family requires temperature=1 — there is no knob to turn this down without leaving the family.

Consequence for the search. Every per-step "gain" in the archived chain was < 0.10. The noise floor is ≥ ±0.07. The architect loop was hill-climbing on a signal it cannot actually resolve. This is the root cause of the "why does our evolution chase noise" question.

4. log.jsonl is not robustly per-episode reconstructable

A 216-ep run's log.jsonl is ~29,200 events from 40 workers interleaved into one file. There is no episode_index, episode_id, or worker_id field on the events — only a step counter. The only episode-identifying information is the instruction text embedded in some node prompts.

carries instruction textdoes not
planner_llm, plan_step, step, reset, init — 100 % iter_in / iter_out, image_budget, evaluate, parse_action, update_history, all viewer_*
Total: 6,834 / 29,219 events = 23 % can be keyed to an episode. The other 77 % have no episode-identifying field at all.

Instruction-as-key works losslessly for the planner-reasoning subset (every R2R instruction is unique — verified 216/216 distinct on MapGPT72), so the "what the agent thought and where it moved" trace can be fully rebuilt. But a complete 14-node-type per-episode log cannot — and the trick relies on a dataset accident (unique instructions), not on the log schema guaranteeing anything.

5. The pipeline does not "trace" logs — it improvises

The decisive finding. We grepped every lib/*.py across all architect variants: 

grep -rln 'log.jsonl' .claude/commands/architect/*/lib/*.py  →  zero hits

No structured parser exists. The evaluation helpers in lib/helpers.py only touch export.json (which is a clean per-episode structure) for the bootstrap CI. The log.jsonl trajectory analysis is a prose instruction in proposer.md: 

Read outputs/eval_runs/{run_id}/log.jsonl for trajectory log
(downsample if > 1 MB). Generate a textual analysis_summary
describing dominant failure modes.

That instruction, plus the log path, is handed to a general-purpose Claude sub-agent which improvises the parsing. The only written-down reconstruction recipe (myloop/analyze.md) is exactly the instruction-as-key method — plus a fragile heuristic to recover the 77 % of unkeyed events:

From myloop/analyze.md, step 3: for tool rows with no instruction, "pair them with the immediately preceding orchestrator_llm row at the same step — timestamp ordering within ~1 second is reliable."

With 40 workers interleaving, "the immediately preceding row" guessed from ±1 s timestamps is not reliable.

And it does not even read the whole run. analyze.md explicitly says: "Don't read all rows. After grouping by episode_id, sample 3 high + 5 failed episodes." The failure-mode analysis that drives the next proposal is built on ~8 of 216 episodes.

6. The full causal chain

This is why the evolution chases noise — it is not one bug, it is a pipeline of lossy stages:

216-ep full trajectories
  → sample only ~8 episodes
  → instruction-key reconstruction (77 % of events unattributable,
       recovered by ±1 s timestamp guessing)
  → feed to a temperature-1 LLM to "find the dominant failure mode"
  → LLM emits an analysis_summary
  → next proposal is conditioned on that summary
  → fitness measured with ±0.07–0.10 noise
  → archive commits the result as if it were signal

Every stage discards information or injects noise. The proposer's view of "why the last iter failed" may itself be sampling bias + reconstruction error + LLM hallucination — and the fitness number that judges its fix cannot resolve gains below 0.10 anyway.

7. Recommendations

#fixaddresses
R1 Add episode_index (and ideally worker_id) to every ExecutionLogger event, or have the eval runner split log.jsonl per episode under assets/<episode_index>/. Finding 4 — makes per-episode reconstruction an O(1) group-by instead of a dataset-dependent accident.
R2 Write a real log.jsonl parser into the architect lib/: deterministic per-episode reconstruction over all 216 episodes, emitting structured stats (fail-step distribution, STOP-decision confusion matrix, loop detection). The LLM then interprets statistics instead of improvising them. Finding 5 & 6 — removes the sampling + improvisation + ±1 s heuristic from the signal path.
R3 Either run N ≥ 3 evals per iter and compare means, or move off the gpt-5 family to one that allows temperature=0 for the evaluated agent. Treat any ΔSR < ~0.10 on a single run as noise, not signal. Findings 1–3 — makes the fitness signal actually resolvable.
